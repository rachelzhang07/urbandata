Clean-Eating in Manhattan¶

Exploring Vegetarian-friendly Restaurants and Neighborhoods¶

Part 1: The Popularity of the Vegetarian Diet¶

In the past decades, people who identify themselves as vegetarian, vegan, or plant-based were viewed as weird and deficient in nutrition. Until the first decade of the 21st century, references to vegetarian eating in social media were often negative. The society is afraid of this plant-based lifestyle.

But now, all that has been changing. Diets that exclude meat, milk, and eggs are recognized as fads. From celebrities to the middle class, this clean-eating diet becomes popular among people. Eating clean is not limited to live a healthier life. Instead, it becomes a movement in an overall generation. Healthy cuisines, colorful ingredients, and lovely photos on Instagram, which could be reasons that people are trying to shift their consuming. According to a report by research firm GlobalData, here’s been a 600% increase in people identifying as vegans in the U.S in the last three years. Only 1% of U.S. consumers claimed to be vegan in 2014. And in 2017, that number rose to 6%. Even Walmart, the world’s largest retailer, is asking its suppliers to offer more plant-based products.

Beyond losing weight and keeping slim, the clean-eating diet has become a fashion among people, especially among millennials. This project will focus on the clean-eating restaurants in New York City: where to go for more vegetarian-friendly restaurants? Where to go for vegetarian-friendly restaurants with the highest rating score? Where to go for a “luxury” clean-eating meal? Let’s choose the “best” vegetarian-friendly restaurant and eat clean together.

Part 2: Exploring Vegetarian-friendly Restaurants in Manhattan¶

(1) Where to go for more vegetarian-friendly restaurants?¶

While searching “Vegetarian-friendly” in Manhattan, on the map, we can find that they are denser in part of Midtown and Downtown Manhattan. But which neighborhoods have more than others? Digging deep by using Yelp API with around 800 restaurants in Manhattan, top neighborhoods where people are easier to find vegetarian food/restaurants are highlighted: Clinton, East Village, Chelsea, and Murray Hill. If you are planning a vacation to New York City, and you also are a fan of healthy food, neighborhoods mentioned above can be ideal choices for a bite. Among the three, at Clinton and East Village, with various categories of dishes, including Mediterranean, Thai, Mexican, and Chinese cuisines, you’ll always find your best matching one. On the contrary, in Inwood, Washington Heights, and Central Harlem, finding vegetarian dishes might be more much difficult. In addition to the total number of “green” restaurants, rates by customers and price level are also important when you would like to find a wonderful eating place.
